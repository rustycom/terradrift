# Rahman et al. — "The Seven Sins: Security Smells in IaC Scripts" (ICSE 2019)

## What they did
Analyzed Puppet manifests for seven recurring security smells (e.g.,
hard-coded secrets, weak crypto, suspicious comments) using static rules
and manual coding.

## Why it matters for TerraDrift
- Establishes the academic legitimacy of empirical IaC security studies.
- Their **smell taxonomy** is methodologically similar to our 17-category
  taxonomy, but Puppet ≠ Terraform and they did **not** measure lifecycle.
- Gap I'm filling: temporal dynamics — how *long* misconfigurations live
  and how *often* they regress after fixes.

## One technique to steal
Their inter-rater agreement protocol (Cohen's κ on a 200-sample subset)
should be reused when validating my taxonomy.

## Open questions
- Does smell *recurrence* track contributor turnover?
- Are smells correlated with test/CI presence?
